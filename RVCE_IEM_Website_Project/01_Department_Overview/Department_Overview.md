## 1. Department History & Evolution

- The Department was **established in 1985** under Bangalore University affiliation, originally oriented toward traditional business/industrial practices. ⚠ (year per third-party aggregators + official narrative)
- In **1992**, it transitioned from traditional business practices to contemporary engineering + management interventions and was **renamed the "Department of Industrial Engineering and Management."** ⚠
- The Department was **accorded Autonomous status by VTU, Belagavi and UGC, New Delhi in 2007.** ✅ (official "About the Department" page)
- The parent institution, **RVCE, was established in 1963** under the Rashtreeya Sikshana Samithi Trust (RSST) — one of India's earliest self-financing engineering colleges — and was granted **autonomous status in 2008.** ✅ (Wikipedia)
- IEM is one of **14 undergraduate engineering departments** at RVCE. ✅ (Wikipedia)

## 10. Why IEM is Unique (positioning)

IEM is a **hybrid of mechanical/industrial engineering and management** — the only RVCE branch that formally blends engineering with functional management (operations, supply chain, quality, finance, HR, information systems). Graduates are positioned as **"generalist engineers"** able to work across manufacturing, services, IT, finance and R&D, and to pursue entrepreneurship. Curriculum spans Mechanical Engineering fundamentals, Industrial Engineering, functional management, Information Systems, Supply Chain Management, Quality Management and quantitative decision models.
