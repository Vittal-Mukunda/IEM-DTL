## 3. Career Paths — "What can an IEM graduate do?"

IEM is uniquely cross-disciplinary, so graduates qualify for **both core-engineering and management/analytics roles**:

**Operations & Supply Chain:** Operations Analyst, Supply Chain Analyst, Logistics/Procurement Executive, Production Planning & Control (PPC) Engineer, Inventory Analyst, Demand Planner.
**Quality & Process:** Quality Engineer, Six Sigma/Lean Consultant, Process Improvement Engineer, Industrial Engineer, Manufacturing Engineer, Ergonomics/Methods Engineer.
**Management Consulting & Analytics:** Business Analyst, Management Consultant, Data/Operations Research Analyst, Project Management Office (PMO) roles.
**IT & Product:** ERP Consultant (SAP), Product Analyst, Systems Analyst.
**Finance:** Risk/Operations Analyst (banks, trading firms like Futures First, Fidelity).
**Entrepreneurship:** Founder/Co-founder — the program explicitly nurtures entrepreneurial competency.

> *These role mappings are standard IE&M outcomes consistent with the recruiter mix; cross-check specific alumni roles in `07_Alumni`.* ⚠ partially inferential.

## 4. Higher-Education Pathways
- **MBA** (operations, supply chain, analytics, general management) — IIMs / top B-schools (CAT/GMAT).
- **MS / M.Tech** abroad or in India — Industrial Engineering, Operations Research, Supply Chain, Engineering Management, Data Science (GRE/GATE).
- **PhD / Research** — including via RVCE's own VTU-recognized research centre.
- IEM's quantitative + management blend makes it a strong feeder for **analytics and operations-management graduate programs.**
