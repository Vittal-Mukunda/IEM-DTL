## 1. Department overview — UPDATED
- **History (refined):** programme **introduced in 1980**, became an **independent department in 1985**, **renamed "Industrial Engineering and Management" in 1992**.
- **Programmes offered:** **B.E. (IEM)** + **M.Sc (Engg.)** + **Ph.D.** — all under VTU; UG is autonomous & **NBA-accredited**.
- **Approved UG intake: 60** *(up from 50 in older data)*.
- **Tagline:** "Designing the Future of Industry" / "Where Management Meets Manufacturing Excellence."
- **Pedagogy:** Outcome-Based Education (OBE); project-based learning, hackathons, tech fests, student clubs.
- **Professional society associations (NEW, expanded):** **IIIE** (Indian Institution of Industrial Engineering), **ORSI** (Operational Research Society of India), **IIMM** (Indian Institute of Materials Management), **SIVAM**, **NIQR** (National Institution for Quality & Reliability), **QCFI** (Quality Circle Forum of India), **ISQ**. *(Older data only listed IIE.)*
- **HoD:** Dr. Rajeswara Rao K. V. S. (Associate Professor & Head).
- Source: https://rvce.edu.in/department/iem/about_the_department/ · https://rvce.edu.in/department/iem/b_e_industrial_engineering_and_management/

### Vision (current)
"Imparting innovation and value based education in Industrial Engineering and Management for steering organizations to global standards with an emphasis on sustainable and inclusive development."

### Mission (current, 4 points)
1. Impart scientific knowledge, engineering and managerial skills for driving organizations to global excellence.
2. Promote a culture of training, consultancy, research and entrepreneurship interventions among the students.
3. Institute collaborative academic and research exchange programs with national and globally renowned academia, industries and other organizations.
4. Establish and nurture **centres of excellence** in the niche areas of Industrial and Systems Engineering.

### PEOs (confirmed, current)
- **PEO1** Conceive, design, implement and operate integrated systems, focus on appropriate measures of performance at strategic, tactical and operational levels.
- **PEO2** Develop competency to adapt to changing roles for achieving organizational excellence.
- **PEO3** Design and develop sustainable technologies and solutions for betterment of society.
- **PEO4** Pursue entrepreneurial venture with a focus on creativity and innovation for developing newer products, processes and systems.

### PSOs (current wording)
1. Design, develop, implement and improve integrated systems that involve people, materials, information, equipment and energy.
2. Apply statistical methods, simulation tools, optimisation techniques and metaheuristics to analyse systems and support effective decision making.
3. Understand and demonstrate the engineering aspects of management tasks such as planning, organisation, leadership and control, along with the human element across various sectors of the economy.
